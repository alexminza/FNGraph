Arcsin Function

Returns the arcsine, or inverse sine, of a number. The arcsine is the angle whose sine is number. The returned angle is given in radians in the range -pi/2 to pi/2.

Syntax

arcsin(number)

Number   is any valid numeric expression representing the sine of the angle you want and must be from -1 to 1.

Remarks

If your argument is in degrees, multiply it by pi/180 or use the radians function to convert it to radians.

Example

Expression Description (Result)
arcsin(-0.5) Arcsine of -0.5 in radians, -pi/6 (-0.5236)
arcsin(-0.5)*180/pi Arcsine of -0.5 in degrees (-30)
degrees(arcsin(-0.5)) Arcsine of -0.5 in degrees (-30)
